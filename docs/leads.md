# ArcadeGhosts Lead Operating Manual

## Ideal Customer Profile

ArcadeGhosts is a strong fit for small businesses and organizations that have outgrown ad hoc operations but do not need enterprise software.

Best-fit buyers:

- Owner-operators
- Operations managers
- Office managers
- Practice administrators
- Program directors
- Small team leaders who directly feel workflow friction

Best-fit engagements:

- internal dashboards
- workflow automation
- custom forms and intake systems
- spreadsheet replacements
- reporting tools
- request or approval tracking
- AI-assisted internal tools with clear business constraints

## Target Industries

Prioritize these early industries:

- professional services
- property management and trades
- nonprofits
- small medical and wellness practices with HIPAA caution
- small manufacturers and operations-heavy teams

Deprioritize:

- large enterprises with procurement-heavy sales cycles
- heavily regulated projects that immediately require formal compliance scope
- businesses with no visible process pain or no reachable decision-maker

## Lead Sources

Good early lead sources:

- local and regional business directories
- Chamber or association member lists
- company websites and contact pages
- LinkedIn company and staff pages
- nonprofit staff and board pages
- Google Maps and service listings
- referrals and warm-intro paths
- prior coworkers, community groups, and local network overlap

Use public information only. Do not guess at contact names, direct emails, or job roles.

## Qualification Criteria

Score each lead on:

- visible operational pain
- likely dependence on spreadsheets, forms, email, or manual handoff
- reachability of a decision-maker
- budget likelihood
- urgency or repeated workflow volume
- low-compliance path to a first engagement
- clarity of a small paid first project

## Enrichment Checklist

Collect and verify:

- company name
- website
- industry
- business type
- city and state
- contact person
- title
- public contact method
- phone if public
- LinkedIn profile
- company LinkedIn
- operational pain signal
- likely workflow problem
- specific outreach angle
- suggested first offer
- estimated fit
- confidence score
- priority score
- source URLs
- verification date

Leave unknown fields blank until verified.

## Outreach Principles

- Work in small batches.
- Contact only the best 3 to 5 leads from each research cycle.
- Lead with the operational problem, not the technology stack.
- Keep the message short, specific, and respectful.
- Offer one practical next step, not a giant transformation.
- Log every contact and set the next action immediately.

## First-Offer Strategy

The first offer should be small, concrete, and easy to understand:

- workflow review
- intake tracker prototype
- spreadsheet-to-dashboard concept
- reporting automation sketch
- admin dashboard idea
- request or task tracker prototype

The offer should create momentum toward a paid discovery or prototype, not a vague "let's build software" conversation.

## Follow-Up Cadence

Recommended cadence:

1. Send a tailored first message.
2. Follow up 5 to 7 business days later with a short reminder or a clearer example.
3. Follow up once more 5 to 7 business days after that if the fit is still strong.
4. Move the lead to nurture or archive if there is still no signal.

Do not keep pushing low-signal leads indefinitely.

## Discovery Call Process

Use discovery to understand:

- current workflow
- bottlenecks and duplicate work
- who uses the process
- volume and frequency
- deadline pressure
- budget comfort
- constraints, especially compliance and approvals

End the call with one clear next step:

- no-fit close
- paid discovery
- lightweight proposal
- send follow-up ideas

## Proposal Process

Each proposal should translate the workflow pain into:

- a scoped outcome
- a small set of deliverables
- a realistic timeline
- a price anchored to business value
- assumptions and constraints
- the next decision needed from the client

Avoid over-specifying the system before the real workflow is understood.

## Post-Project Referral Loop

After a successful project:

- request a short testimonial
- ask for one or two referral introductions
- document the before/after workflow improvement
- save screenshots or sanitized examples for future case-study use
- identify adjacent workflows that could become a second project

## Metrics To Track

- new leads added
- leads fully researched
- leads moved to ready to contact
- outreach sent
- response rate
- discovery calls booked
- proposals sent
- proposal win rate
- projects won
- average project value
- best-performing industries
- common objections
